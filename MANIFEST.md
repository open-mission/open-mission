# The Open Mission Manifesto

---

## The Church Was Never Called to Compete

There is a pattern that repeats itself across many organizations.

A team discovers something that works. A process. A system. A way of doing things.
And then, quietly, it becomes a secret.

Not out of malice. But out of habit.
The habit of thinking that what we built belongs to us.

In the corporate world, this makes sense.
Competition is real. Advantage matters.

But the Church is not a corporation.

The Church was never called to compete.
It was called to cooperate.

Every church, every ministry, every missionary, every pastor working in a small town
or a major city — they are all on the same mission.

To share the good news of Jesus Christ.

When we guard our processes, our tools, our knowledge —
we are not protecting the Kingdom.
We are limiting it.

---

## What We Observed

We looked at how churches operate — and we noticed something.

Many communities have developed remarkable things.
Creative ways to welcome visitors.
Systems to care for their members.
Methods to organize volunteers and train leaders.
Ways to study Scripture that genuinely help people grow.

And most of it never leaves the building.

Not because anyone decided to hide it.
But because no one built the infrastructure to share it.

We also looked at the tools churches use every day.

Many of them are expensive.
Many of them are closed.
Many of them are built without the Church in mind.

Small churches, missionaries working in remote areas,
ministries operating with limited resources —
they are left to figure it out on their own,
reinventing what others have already built,
paying for what should be freely available.

---

## The Knowledge That Gets Lost

There is another kind of loss that happens quietly, over time.

A pastor spends decades learning how to lead a community well.
How to welcome someone for the first time.
How to handle conflict with grace.
How to build a team that lasts.
How to recover when something fails.

And when that pastor moves on, most of what they learned moves with them.

Not written down. Not shared. Not passed forward.

A new pastor arrives and starts from the beginning —
making the same mistakes, solving the same problems,
learning the same lessons that were already learned somewhere else.

This is not a technology problem.
It is a culture problem.

The best software communities in the world have learned something important:
when things go wrong, you write it down.
You share what happened, what you tried, what failed, what worked.
Not to assign blame — but so that others don't have to repeat the same journey.

They call it building in public.
We believe the Church can do the same.

Imagine a young pastor with access to the honest reflections
of those who came before —
not polished success stories,
but real accounts of what worked, what didn't, and what they wish they had known.

Imagine a small church in a remote region
being able to adopt a model developed by a community on the other side of the world,
adapted to their own language and culture.

Imagine ministries learning from each other across borders,
because someone decided that their experience was worth sharing.

This is what shared knowledge makes possible.
And this is part of what Open Mission wants to build.

---

## We Believe

We believe technology should serve people — not limit them.

We believe knowledge grows when it is shared.

We believe the same spirit that drives open-source software —
building together, sharing freely, improving collectively —
is deeply consistent with what the Church is called to be.

We believe small churches deserve the same quality of tools
as large institutions.

We believe missionaries in remote regions deserve access
to the same biblical resources available to theologians in major cities.

We believe a new pastor should be able to learn from the experience
of those who came before — including their failures.

We believe that when one community solves a problem,
others should be able to benefit from that solution.

We believe open collaboration is not just a development strategy.
It is a way of serving.

---

## What Open Mission Is

Open Mission is not a software company.

It is not a startup trying to disrupt the Christian technology market.

Open Mission is a community.

A community that believes the Church grows stronger
when it shares knowledge, tools, and technology openly.

We build software. But software is not the goal.

The goal is to lower barriers.
To make biblical resources more accessible.
To give communities the tools they need to serve people well.
To create space for the Church to learn from itself.

We start with Open Bible —
a modern, free, open-source Bible application
built for anyone who wants to engage with Scripture.

But we are building toward something larger:
an open ecosystem of technology, knowledge, and shared experience
that any church, ministry, developer, educator, or pastor
can use, adapt, and build upon.

---

## How We Build

We build in public.

Our code, our discussions, our decisions, our mistakes — all of it, visible.

We believe transparency is not a vulnerability. It is a strength.

We build for the long term.

Not for demos. Not for launches.
For communities that will depend on what we create for years to come.

We build together.

No single person owns this project.
No single organization controls it.
Open Mission belongs to everyone who believes in its mission.

We build with excellence.

Open source is not an excuse for poor quality.
We believe that building something well is itself an act of service.

---

## Who This Is For

This is for the developer who wants their work to matter beyond a paycheck.

This is for the pastor of a small church who cannot afford the tools larger churches take for granted.

This is for the new pastor who wants to learn from the experience of those who came before —
including what they got wrong.

This is for the experienced pastor who has learned things worth sharing
but never had a place to share them.

This is for the missionary who needs Scripture in an offline environment,
in a language that the major platforms do not support.

This is for the translator working to make the Bible accessible
to communities that have never had it in their own language.

This is for the educator who wants open, high-quality resources
to teach the next generation.

This is for anyone who believes that technology and knowledge,
used with humility and generosity,
can be a powerful way to serve others.

---

## An Invitation

We are in the early stages.

There is much to build. Much to learn. Much to share.

If you believe what we believe —
that the Church grows stronger when it cooperates,
that knowledge should be freely shared,
that what one community learns belongs to all,
that technology should remove barriers, not create them —

then Open Mission is yours too.

Not just to use. To build.
