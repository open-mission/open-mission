# MISSION

# Our Mission

Open Mission exists to build open-source technology that makes biblical resources more accessible to everyone.

We believe churches, ministries, educators, missionaries, researchers, and individuals should have access to modern, high-quality software without unnecessary financial or technical barriers.

Our mission is to foster an ecosystem of open collaboration where technology serves people, strengthens communities, and supports engagement with Scripture.

---

# Our Vision

We envision a world where biblical technology is open, accessible, interoperable, and community-driven.

Rather than building isolated applications, we aim to create reusable software, open standards, shared libraries, and public datasets that empower others to build tools of their own.

Open Mission seeks to become a trusted foundation for Christian open-source software.

---

# Why We Exist

Many valuable digital resources remain inaccessible because they are proprietary, expensive, or locked into closed ecosystems.

We believe open-source software can change this reality.

By sharing code, knowledge, and data, we enable communities around the world to adapt technology to their own languages, cultures, and ministries.

Technology should empower local communities—not limit them.

---

# Our Principles

## Faith-Inspired

Open Mission is inspired by Christian values and exists to support biblical engagement.

While our mission is Christian, we welcome contributions from anyone who respects the goals and values of the project.

---

## Open by Default

Whenever possible, our code, documentation, discussions, and decisions are developed in public.

Transparency creates better software and stronger communities.

---

## Community Before Code

Healthy communities build healthy software.

We value respect, collaboration, humility, and constructive discussion above individual preferences.

---

## Simplicity

We prefer simple, maintainable solutions over unnecessary complexity.

Good software should be understandable, sustainable, and easy to contribute to.

---

## Accessibility

Technology should be available regardless of financial resources, location, or technical background.

Whenever possible, our projects should remain free and accessible.

---

## Sustainability

Open-source projects should outlive their original creators.

We design our software, documentation, and governance to encourage long-term community stewardship.

---

## Excellence

Open source is not an excuse for poor quality.

We strive to build software that is reliable, elegant, well-documented, and enjoyable to use.

---

# What We Build

Open Mission develops and maintains projects that contribute to an open ecosystem for biblical technology, including:

* Applications
* Open datasets
* APIs
* Shared libraries
* Developer tools
* Documentation
* Open standards

Each project should strengthen the ecosystem as a whole.

---

# Our Commitment

Every decision should move us closer to a future where biblical resources are freely available, communities can build upon shared foundations, and technology serves people with excellence, humility, and openness.

Open Mission is more than a collection of repositories.

It is a long-term commitment to collaboration, stewardship, and service.
